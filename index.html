About This Site

I started this site as a place to think in public about economics and finance — the kind of thinking that doesn't fit neatly into a classroom or a slide deck. I'm an engineer by training, which means I tend to approach economic questions the way I'd approach an optimization problem: define the objective, map the constraints, and be honest about the uncertainty.

What interests me most is the boundary between the quantitative and the qualitative — where supply chain network models meet monetary policy, where logistics data illuminates consumer behavior, where operations research tools can sharpen macroeconomic intuition.

I grew up in Seoul, spent time in the US and Singapore, and served in the ROK Army as a logistics officer and interpreter. Each of those experiences left me more curious about how economies actually work in practice, not just in theory.
